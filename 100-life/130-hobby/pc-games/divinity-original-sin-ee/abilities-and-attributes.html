Because some traits are clearly better (generally those that provide immunities like Blunt, Forgiving and Spiritual), you MIGHT want to min-max the Trait system.

Abilities

Abilities become more expensive with higher ranks:

Rank Cost for that Rank Total Cost to reach that Rank
1 1 1
2 2 3
3 3 6
4 4 10
5 5 15

This cost structure means that dipping around (i.e. buying 1 rank in a several fields) is relatively cheap, and can be done to improve the build's overall power.

Skill Abilities & Skills

Some Abilities are Skill Abilities. If you've invested in the Man-At-Arms ability, you can then learn the Battering Ram Skill.

Besides being divided according to a skill tree, Skills are also divided into 3 tiers: Novice, Adept and Master.

Rank Skills
1 3 Novice
2 5 Novice, 2 Adept
3 6 Novice, 3 Adept
4 6 Novice, 4 Adept, 1 Master
5 6 Novice, 4 Adept, 2 Master

If you try to use a skill while your Ability Ranks are below the "recommended" rank, then that skill will cost 2 extra AP

Considering that the 5th rank also costs 5 Ability points, taking the 5th rank is generally not worth it.
(One notable exception being Expert Marksman, because it has 2 killer Master skills: Arrow Spray & Rain of Arrows.)

Skill Abilities only determine what skills you can learn, and how much AP they cost. They do not affect how powerful those skills are.

Note that vendors do not stock all possible skillbooks. Some can only be obtained via loot. Others can and should be crafted.

Main Party Abilities

Weapon Abilities

  • The Str Warrior will want to buy up either Two Handed (Madora has 2 ranks) or Dual Wield
  • The Ranger will want to buy up Bow, which I recommend over Crossbow. (Skill attacks do not crit, so the Crossbow skill crit bonus is wasted when using skill attacks)
  • Tenebrium: You can get 1 free point in this from a quest or a book, no further investment recommended and needed (end game enemies are immune or healed by Tenebrium.)

Bodybuilding and Willpower

  • Bodybuilding protects against: Knock Down, Burning, Frozen, Bleeding, Crippled, Blind, Weak, Disease, Infected, & Drunk.
  • Willpower protects against: Fear, Mute, Charm, Stun, Curse, Slow, Petrify, Decaying Touch, Mark of Death, & Remorse.

Each rank reduces the chance of being affected by 15%

Armor Specialization

  • each rank provides a +3 Armor Penalty
  • each rank lowers the movement penalty for Heavy Armor by 20% (you can combine any Heavy Armor with a Bottle of Oil to remove the movement penalty.)
  • Armor Specialist is required for some of the end game's heavy armor (between 1-3 ranks)

Lore

  • Provides you with more information about the enemy

Downtime Companions

The game provides 4 companions, of which only 2 can be in your party. Other 2 can be turned into downtime companions. Have them buy abilities & talents (which should NOT be taken by your main party), and equip them with gear, which you only need in safe location.

Crafting & Blacksmith

  • 2 very similar abilities. that allows you to create/upgrade/craft items and consumables
  • Both Crafting and Blacksmithing gain a +1 from the Scientist talent

Wolgraff and Baidotr recommended as downtime companions

  • Baidotr should fill Crafting & Blacksmith role.
  • Wolgraff will be dedicated to Lockpicking and Pickpocket.

Blacksmith is also used to repair items. In the early game, you need at least Blacksmith 1. Initially, Jahan can fill this role, thanks to his Scientist talent

Barter

You only barter with Merchants

  • Wolgraff is best suited for this role (since he will also be pickpocketing the merchants).
  • Baidotr has a trait which gives her a penalty to Attitude.

Lockpicking

Lockpick is strictly not necessary on you main party of 4.

If Wolgraff is one of your downtime companions, he comes with 1 rank in Lockpicking.

Pickpocket

It isn't necessary to Pickpocket every merchant you come across.

If Wolgraff is one of your downtime companions, he comes with 1 rank in Pickpocket.

Sneak

In DOS-EE, sneak in combat costs 5 AP regardless of your Sneak ranks.
You will want to hold onto a set of sneak buffing gear (usually helmet and boots). You can also craft your own sneak boost gear by adding Void Essence to armor.

Attributes

Skill power is usually determined by your Attributes (some skills, notably Summoning, don't rely on Attributes at all). For weapon users passive Weapon Abilities also determine bonus damage. Attributes also determine the skill's cooldown (some skills can have their cooldown reduced to 1 turn after 15 Attribute rank)

By level 20, you will have 15 Attribute points to spend, which is enough to max out 1 Attribute, and spread some points (given by magic gear) in other areas

Strength (Str)

  • Increases the chance-to-hit for Str based weapons (swords, axes, clubs, spears)
  • determines the effectiveness and cooldown for Man-At-Arms skills
  • gives +20 carrying capacity per point.

Important for Warriors.

Dexterity (Dex)

  • Increases the chance-to-hit for Dex based weapons (daggers and bows)
  • determines the effectiveness and cooldown for Expert Marksman and Scoundrel skills.

Important for Rangers and Scoundrels,

Intelligence (Int)

  • Increases the chance-to-hit for Int based weapons (staff)
  • determines the effectiveness and cooldown for: Hydrosophist, Pyrokinetic, Geomancer, Aerotheurge and Witchcraft skills.

Important for Mages

Constitution (Con)

  • +1 Maximum Action Point capacity per point (starts at 7).
  • +6.2 Vitality per point and level.

Speed (Spd)

  • +0.1 Movement per point
  • +0.5 Initiative per point
  • +0.5 starting Action Points per point (starts at 2)
  • +0.5 Turn Action Points per point (starts at 3.5)

Speed should be an odd number to get its full benefits.
At 5 Spd you have 6 AP per turn, and every 2 Spd above that grants you an additional 1 AP per turn.

Perception (Per)

  • +1.0 Hearing per point
  • +0.5 Initiative per point
  • +0.5 starting Action Points per point (starts at 2)
  • +1% Critical Chance per point above 5
  • Improved ability to detect traps
  • Accuracy when shooting over long distances (important for Rangers)

You will want at least 1 party member to have decent perception

  • The Party Scout should start with the Light Stepper talent and 6 Per, and add 1 point every 8 levels (7 Per @ L8, 8 Per @ L 16).
  • Have the Mage cast Burn My Eyes on the Scout at all times (+2 Per boost). And wear magic gear that boosts Perception.

Putting it Together

Pure Vs Hybrid

For a beginners, I recommend making "pure" classes, because they are easier to learn, and harder to screw up.
The main limiting factor in making a hybrid are your Attributes

Skills Dips

Some skills DO NOT rely on Attributes. Anyone can dip into these Skill Abilities, learn these skills, and benefit from them

LV1 Geomancer

  • Summon Spider: 8 Legged Tank
  • Midnight Oil: Large patch of Oil. Combo with Firestorm Grenades
  • Boulder Bash: Low Intelligence (Int) reduces damage, but can still apply Oiled to opponents.

LV1 Aerotheurge

  • Wind of Change: Removes Stun, Slow and Petrify.
  • Teleport: Low Intelligence (Int) reduces damage, but can still move enemies (and allies) around, making it excellent Crowd Control. There are some segments in the game where you have to teleport an ally around to solve puzzles, where dealing less damage is an advantage.
  • Blitz Bolt: Low Intelligence (Int) reduces damage and stun chance, but can still be used to deal Air damage. Good for electrifying puddles.

LV1 Hydrosophist

  • Rain: Creates puddles. Despite the description, it will make everything Wet all the time, even with low Int.
  • Regenerate: Low Intelligence (Int) reduces amount healed
  • Avatar of Frost: Low Intelligence (Int) reduces the chance of successfully applying this buff, but it is the only skill in your arsenal that can remove the Frozen condition.

LV1 Witchcraft

  • Summon Undead Warrior: Bony Tank
  • Oath of Desecration: Low Intelligence (Int) reduces the chance of successfully applying this buff.
  • Vampiric Touch: Low Intelligence (Int) reduces the life stolen.

LV1 Scoundrel

  • Walk in Shadows: Invisibility. Low Dexterity (Dex) reduces the chance of successfully applying this buff.
  • Winged Feet: Ignore Terrain, including lava. Low Dexterity (Dex) reduces the chance of successfully applying this buff. Very useful for the Warrior, who might otherwise be hobbled by dangerous terrain.
  • Fast Track: Haste. Low Dexterity (Dex) reduces the chance of successfully applying this buff.

Getting a Third Summon

 By the end of the game, a non-mage will likely want a third summon.
There are 2 alternate choices here

  1. Take the 2nd rank of Hydrosophist, learn all the Novice skills along with Summon Ice Elemental and Ice Wall.

    • The Ice Elemental makes a decent tank when the Mage starts throwing out Hail Attack.
    • Ice Wall has a chance of freezing enemies which are caught in the wall, and low Intelligence (Int) penalizes that chance. Regardless of Intelligence (Int), Ice Wall can always be used to block movement.

  2. Quest Reward: summon skillbook, which can be given to any 1 party member. This Summon skill has no Ability rank requirements, so anybody can learn it (you only get one book)

Common Non-Mage Dip Set: 4 Abilities at 1 rank, 1 Ability at 2 ranks (7 Ability Points)

Summoning

  • always summon at the start of combat (and re-summon after summon death)
  • block line of fire with summon if possible (it prevents your characters from being hit)
  • re-summon when only 1 turn left (creature will drop dead at the end of the summoner's turn otherwise)

Defense Abilities

  • All the builds have 3 in Bodybuilding and Willpower.
  • Recommendation: Buy up 1 Bodybuilding/Willpower every 6 levels (e.g. B/W1 @ L6, B/W2 @ L12, B/W3 @ L18).

Miscellaneous

  • Bows/Staves: get at least 1 weapon for each elemental damage (switch depending on target weakness).
  • Melee weapons: in addition to elemental damage, get different physical damage types (Slashing, Piercing, Bludgeoning).
  • Two Handers: spears have very good reach (6m), and Hammers don't appear till late game.
