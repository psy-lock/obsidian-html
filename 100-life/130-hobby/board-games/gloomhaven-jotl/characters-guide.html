Characters Guide

📋 Overview

Red Guard

  • Gloomhaven: Jaws of the Lion - Red Guard 1-9 Guide

Level 1

Swift Strength (224)

This card is a very meh top but a great bottom. Sure the move isn't very high but having an unconditional shield is quite handy. This is a good card to pair with Shield Spikes on turn 1 to get the enemies focusing you and taking some damage.

Healing Sands (225)


This card will be useful even up to level 9. It's a very strong self-heal to pop in between rooms and the bottom action be very handy in mitigating damage. Unless necessary, I would try to use this for the bottom as it can prevent a good amount of damage.

Shield Spikes (226)


As mentioned, our build will be formed around this card. It will nearly always be our first or second card played each scenario. Because of this, we'll never be using the bottom.

Flaming Sickle (227)


This is a meh card. You need to be too close for this to be useful and it's very slow. That being said, it is one of our few fire generating cards early on so it may need to be used.

Blinding Sickle (228)


Same as above. This one may be even worse as we generally want enemies to hit us and immobilizing them doesn't allow them to.

Twirling Stabs (229)


A fairly good use of light. We'll be in the thick of things so it may be that we can hit up to three enemies with this on a regular basis. The bottom is great if we don't need to move on a turn.

Flame Shroud (230)


Move 4 on a 6 initiative is an absolute keeper. We really want to use fire for this if possible

Shocking Advance (231)


Both the top and bottom are really good. We want to use the top when possible but sometimes we'll need to be flexible and use the bottom to generate light. The top is notably very strong in that it can hold ranged enemies next to you and force them into disadvantage AND taking Shield Spikes damage.

Shield of the Desert (232)


If there's a card worth using for its loss in this build, it's this one. If you can get in the middle of a group of enemies and drop this + a shield top, you're going to be taking no damage and blowing through their health. The top is a good ability too when we want to go more on the offensive but it does take away the elements we want to often use defensively.

Desert Night (233)


I would use this in a scenario with bigger enemies that are less plentiful. Disarming a hard-hitting enemy will do you more good than just shielding against one. That being said, in most scenarios, we'll be the focus of multiple attacks, meaning a shield is going to be the better option overall. Why disarm when you can fully negate an attack?

Level X

Warrior of the Sun (234)


Probably my favorite shield card at level 1. While it seems sort of lame it does a lot for us. It locks in ranged enemies (forcing them to hit us with disadvantage), lets us go early in the turn, and generates light for future use. It's a nice setup for Shocking Advance and definitely worth a grab.

Blade Dance (235)


Not really our style. Using two elements to do a couple of weak hits and pull enemies toward us so that they can hit us with no shield up is a bad idea. The bottom loss could be cool but we have a better bottom loss in Shield of the Desert.

Precision Strike (236)


Another good loss that would undoubtedly be nice in boss fights. The bottom, while not giving us a shield, can help us pull in more ranged or melee enemies to hit with our Shield Spikes (226). Nothing special though...

Level 1 and X Card Choice

flexible means it could go either way

Tops

  • Shield Spikes (226)
  • Shocking Advance (231) (flexible)
  • Warrior of the Sun (234)
  • Shield of the Desert (232) (bottom for loss)
  • Flaming Sickle (227)

Bottoms

  • Swift Strength (224)
  • Healing Sands (225) (flexible)
  • Flame Shroud (230)
  • Precision Strike (236)
  • Twirling Stabs (229) or Blinding Sickle (228) (both flexible)

Level 2

Harvest Sickle (237)

Barbaric Instincts (238)


Barbaric Instincts is just so handy, giving us yet another shield option. The pull can be handy if a ranged enemy does not have a move option this turn, and the fire generation is sorely needed if we want to use fire on Twirling Stabs bottom. The bottom of the card is also usable in a pinch but again not our ideal use of light.

Level 2 Card Choice

Barbaric Instincts will have to either replaceFlaming Sickle (227) (good choice if you have some other fire generation) or Shield of the Desert (232) (if you don't want the loss or don't need the damage).

The Favorite

Розіграти карту, після чого покласти на неї Shortened Glossary > Жетон Персонажата одразу отримати досвід (один раз). Під час будьякої дистанційної атаки можна додати +3 шкоди (до значення шкоди без модифікатора атаки), поклавши Shortened Glossary > Жетон Персонажа на обрану ціль. Після смерті цілі, жетон необхідно підібрати з плитки.

Приклад:

  • Базовова шкода: 3
  • Шкода від жетону: 3
  • Модифікатор: 2x
  • Результат: 12 шкоди = (3+3) * 2

Demolitionist

Windup

Для наступних двох атак додати +2 шкоди. Якщо задіяно атаку по області - додаткова шкода застовується тільки до перших двох ворогів.

Приклад:

  • Базовова шкода: 3
  • Шкода від здібності: 2 (два заряди)
  • Цілей в області: 3
  • Модифікатор: для кожної цілі окремо
  • Результат: 1, 2 ціль = 5 шкоди + AM; 3 ворог = 3 шкоди + AM
